Lisp Books and Information

My collection of Lisp books and information. Some links are to freely-distributable PDFs, others to web pages.

Books

Common Lisp: A Gentle Introduction to Symbolic Computation
© 1990 Symbolic Technology Ltd.

Common Lisp: The Language
© 1984 Guy Steele / Digital Press

On Lisp: Advanced Techniques for Common Lisp
© 1993 Paul Graham

Lisp 1.5 Programmer’s Manual
1962 John McCarthy, Paul Abrahams, Daniel Edwards, Timothy Hart, Michael Levin / MIT Press
Courtesy of Paul McJones and his History of LISP project pages at the Computer History Museum

Performance and Evaluation of Lisp Systems
© 1985 Richard P. Gabriel / MIT Press

Available online, but not as PDFs or with licenses that don’t allow mirroring:

Practical Common Lisp by Peter Seibel (my current favorite Lisp book)
Successful Lisp by David Lamkins
Casting SPELs in Lisp by Conrad Barski, M.D.
Common Lisp - An Interactive Approach by Stuart Shapiro
Loving Lisp - the Savy Programmer’s Secret Weapon by Mark Watson
The Common Lisp HyperSpec
The Common Lisp Cookbook
An Introduction and Tutorial for Common Lisp

I’m not including many Scheme titles here, but of course I have to mention the classic
Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs from MIT Press (HTML, PDF).
Video lectures to accompany the text are available from MIT and Ars Digita University.

The Little Schemer is an update of “The Little LISPer”.
How To Design Programs is a great book from MIT Press, available online as HTML.

Emacs is the Lisp editor of choice.
The GNU Emacs Manual
Introduction to Emacs Lisp Programming
Emacs Lisp Reference Manual
Marco Baringer made a video showing the use of SLIME, the Superior Lisp Interaction Mode for Emacs.
I’m gradually switching to ERC, the Emacs IRC Client

Symbolics Lisp Machines

I’ve long been fascinated by Lisp Machines, especially those made by Symbolics.

I’m currently in the market for a Symbolics system (preferrably a MacIvory board due to size and power considerations, but I’ll take what I can get).
Please contact me if you can help.
